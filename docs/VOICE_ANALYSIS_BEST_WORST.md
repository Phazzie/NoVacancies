## Pattern Analysis

### What Makes Lines GOOD:
1. ‚úÖ **Specific numbers/details** - "forty dollars," "six hundred," "every hour on the hour"
2. ‚úÖ **Contractions** - "can't," "doesn't," "that's" (conversational rhythm)
3. ‚úÖ **Visual/concrete actions** - "throws wrapper on floor," "rides his bike"
4. ‚úÖ **Dark humor through metaphor** - "in collections," "subsidy," "gets the movie"
5. ‚úÖ **Economy** - Short, punchy, no waste ("You want witnesses")
6. ‚úÖ **Sydney's transactional lens** - Financial/logistical language for emotional reality

### What Makes Lines BAD:
1. ‚ùå **Abstract statements** - "entitlement is starting to show" (tell, don't show)
2. ‚ùå **Expanded contractions** - "cannot," "does not," "it is not" (stiff, corporate)
3. ‚ùå **Missing specifics** - Removed numbers, objects, timelines
4. ‚ùå **Generic conclusions** - "the clock does not care" (flat)
5. ‚ùå **Technical language** - "mock fallback path," "themes of codependency"
6. ‚ùå **Lost narratives** - Removed micro-stories that teach through showing

## The Core Problem

**Someone "cleaned up" the prose for clarity/professionalism and killed the voice in the process.**

The degraded lines are more "correct" (formal contractions, complete subjects) but less TRUE. They sound like a requirements document, not a functional meth addict counting rent money at 6:47 AM.

**Sydney's voice is:**
- Transactional (financial metaphors)
- Precise (specific numbers, times, objects)
- Dry/bitter (dark humor as coping)
- Observant (records behavior, doesn't moralize)
- Exhausted (short sentences when tired)

**The degraded lines are:**
- Generic (abstract observations)
- Formal (expanded contractions)
- Corporate ("does not," "cannot")
- Moralistic (conclusions instead of evidence)
- Academic ("themes of," "deals with")

---

## The Fix

**Restore specificity. Restore contractions. Restore Sydney.**

Every line should pass the test: "Would a functional meth addict at 6:47 AM think this exact phrase?" If no, it's not voice ceiling.
